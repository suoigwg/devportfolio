About Me

Hi! I'm Yisheng Yang, currently a graduate student in the electrical and computer engineering department at University of Illinois, Urbana Champaign. Previously, I received my bachelor's degree in software engineering from ECNU in 2018.

Ever since I was in eleventh grade, software engineering has been my passion. I still remember the first program I wrote, with the PASCAL . I was so excited when it printed out 'Hello World'. It was almost like magic, being able to get the computer to do whatever I wanted, just by typing in these funny little characters. Throughout my college life, I latched on to any ideas for projects I could get, moving from "Hello World" all the way to a simple online web store, with dozens of projects in between. I learned Java, Go and Python, tearing through any books I could find on anything remotely related to software development. I was attracted.

This passion carried me through my education at UIUC. Here there were more resources available to me than I can ever image as a teenager. I took classes on databases, blockchains and parallel programming, all with the same curiosity the drove me in my eleventh grade. And during this period I enjoyed my first internship in the Silicon Valley.

I am perceived as a hard-working person by my peers and managers. During my internship at Snowflake, my mentor said that I was a great team player and always try to do things to the best I can. At school, my classmates like to work on projects with me. I often come up with great insights about the problem and I always listen. I enjoy working with others and I believe my friends and colleagues feel the same.

I am graduating this December and currently looking for entry level jobs as a software engineer. With my solid knowledge of software development and great interpersonal skills, I believe I will be a good fit for most technology companies.
